Case Report
Summary

Case in Brief

A 53-year-old man presented with a slow-growing, painless left preauricular swelling over 1.5 years, initially suspected to be a parotid tumour. He had no past medical history, no chronic cough, no TB contacts, and two normal chest radiographs.

Ultrasound (×2) localised an intra-parotid lymph node. FNA cytology showed non-caseating granulomas with a negative AFB stain, excluding benign neoplasms and favouring sarcoidosis. MRI demonstrated two homogeneously enhancing superficial-lobe lesions with no central necrosis — reported as "nothing sinister". The diagnosis was reached only when FNA culture grew Mycobacterium tuberculosis, confirming primary intra-parotid TB. The patient was started on anti-TB therapy and parotidectomy was avoided.

Key message

Normal CXR · negative AFB · non-caseating FNA · benign MRI — yet TB. No single test was sufficient; holistic multimodal correlation made the diagnosis.

Take Home

Key Learning Points

  • 1Primary parotid TB exists — a normal chest radiograph does not exclude TB in an indeterminate intra-parotid mass.
  • 2Negative AFB ≠ no TB — Ziehl-Neelsen smear positivity is only ~27% in TB lymphadenitis; mycobacterial culture is essential.
  • 3FNA may miss caseation — non-caseating granulomas can reflect a sampling limitation, not a true absence of caseation.
  • 4Benign MRI ≠ benign diagnosis — homogeneous, non-necrotic enhancement is an atypical but recognised TB pattern; low T2 signal is a subtle clue.
  • 5Always send culture when granulomas are found in a parotid lesion — here it was the only definitive test.
